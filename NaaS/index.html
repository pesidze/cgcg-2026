The Concept

Your network.
Without the headaches.

Network as a Service (NaaS) is a cloud-based model where your entire network infrastructure — routers, switches, firewalls, SD-WAN, and monitoring — is delivered as a fully managed monthly service.

Instead of buying expensive hardware, hiring specialized staff, or managing complex vendor relationships, you get a fast, secure, always-updated network with a single point of accountability.

Buyer's Guide

What to look for in a NaaS provider.

1

Local presence

Your NaaS provider should have engineers who can be on-site in your New York office within hours — not a remote-only team handling your business over a chat window.

2

Transparency in SLAs

Understand exactly what uptime guarantees, response times, and remediation processes you're signing up for. Ask for written SLAs before signing anything.

3

No vendor lock-in

A good NaaS provider uses hardware-agnostic architectures and won't trap you in proprietary equipment that you can't move if you change providers.

4

Security-first design

Every NaaS deployment should include firewall management, intrusion detection, and network segmentation as standard — not as paid add-ons.

5

Integration capability

Your network is the foundation of everything else. Your provider should integrate NaaS with your VoIP, ERP, security cameras, and cloud services from day one.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions.

What is Network as a Service (NaaS)?

NaaS is a cloud-based delivery model for networking infrastructure. Instead of buying and managing your own routers, switches, and firewalls, you subscribe to a fully managed network from a provider like CGCG. You get a faster, more secure network for a fixed monthly cost — with no capital expense and no ongoing hardware headaches.

How much does NaaS cost for a small business in New York?

Pricing depends on your space size, number of users, required uptime, and compliance needs. Most CGCG NaaS clients pay between $300 and $1,500/month — significantly less than managing hardware, software licenses, and IT labor separately. Contact us for a free network assessment and quote.

Will switching to NaaS require downtime?

We design all migrations to minimize disruption. Most transitions are completed within a scheduled maintenance window of 2–4 hours after business hours. We've never failed a migration — and we document every step so you know exactly what's happening and when.

Is NaaS secure enough for regulated industries like healthcare, law, and finance?

Yes. CGCG's NaaS architecture includes enterprise-grade firewall management, intrusion detection, network segmentation, and end-to-end encryption as standard. We design every deployment to meet HIPAA, SOX, NY SHIELD Act, and financial services compliance requirements by default — not as an add-on.

Do I need to buy any hardware?

In most NaaS deployments, hardware is included in your monthly subscription. We install and own the equipment — you get the performance and reliability without the capital expense or asset management burden. If you have existing hardware you'd like to retain, we'll assess it during your free network evaluation.
