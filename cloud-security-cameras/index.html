Why It Matters

Why your camera system needs to be right

Poor camera placement is one of the most common reasons surveillance footage fails to protect a business. The cases below happen when systems are installed without a plan.

01

Detect and Stop Incidents Before They Escalate

AI-powered analytics send alerts based on specific activities — unauthorized entry after hours, loitering in restricted areas, unattended packages, or vehicles in a no-access zone. You don't have to review hours of footage to know something happened. The system tells you when, and shows you where.

02

Reduce Liability and Insurance Exposure

Clear, accessible, timestamped recordings give you documentation to disprove false claims from employees, customers, or vendors. Well-documented surveillance systems can also reduce commercial property and liability insurance premiums — a direct financial return on the installation investment.

03

Security Guard Efficiency

For businesses with on-site security personnel, integrated surveillance dramatically improves coverage. Guards can monitor multiple areas simultaneously through a central VMS dashboard, respond faster to incidents with accurate location data, and document events with synchronized camera timestamps.

What We Deliver

Installed right. Managed continuously.

From the site survey to ongoing firmware management, CGCG handles every layer of your surveillance infrastructure — including a VMS that unifies all locations into a single interface.

Thoughtful Installation

CGCG's installation team carefully selects, places, and configures every component to ensure continuous, gap-free coverage — no blind spots at entry points, no glare from windows, no cameras positioned too high for facial recognition, and no storage that runs out before an incident is reported.

Once the system is live, our team continues working in the background: applying firmware updates, monitoring for system anomalies, and ensuring you're getting the maximum coverage your infrastructure supports.

Video Management Software (VMS)

Every deployment includes a VMS layer that captures, organizes, and stores footage from all cameras in a unified interface — regardless of how many locations or providers your current setup involves.

  • Unified dashboard across all locations and camera feeds
  • Continuous recording with no gaps in coverage
  • Extensive video archives — from months to years of historical footage depending on storage configuration
  • Remote access to live and historical feeds from anywhere — New York to Dubai
  • Legacy device compatibility — CGCG's VMS works with cameras installed by previous providers, protecting your existing hardware investment

Cloud-based storage, on-premise storage, or a hybrid of both are all available depending on your compliance requirements, budget, and retention needs.

The Bigger Picture

A camera system that operates in isolation is a reactive tool.

A camera system connected to your access control, your network monitoring, and your IT infrastructure is a proactive one. CGCG installs and manages security cameras as part of a unified infrastructure — when a door access event triggers a camera flag at 2 AM, you don't need three different vendors to tell you what happened. One partner handles the whole picture.

01
Cameras connect to your access control
Door access events and camera flags correlate automatically — so an unauthorized credential attempt at 2 AM is immediately paired with footage from every camera covering that entry point. No manual cross-referencing.
02
Cameras integrate with your network monitoring
IP cameras operate on your managed network infrastructure. CGCG monitors the health of your camera network alongside your other managed IT systems — so a camera going offline is treated as a security event, not a mystery.
03
Cameras feed into your cybersecurity stack
Physical security events and digital security events are often connected. When camera data, access logs, and network anomalies are monitored by a single partner, the correlation happens automatically — and the response is faster.
